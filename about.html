Who I Am

I will admit that programming was not my first field of choice. I'd always been interested in it, but the amount of time and work it seemed to demand put me off for a good while. Most of my school and college career was centered around the Arts and Sciences; I graduated college with a degree in evolutionary biology and ecology, not computer programming. But after a few attempted jobs, I found that I performed best when my brain had something to chew on. I was much better - and happier - at creating BWA pipelines than I was collecting samples in the field.

In August of 2025, I was employed as a data analyst and AI engineer. I learned two very important things during that time:

  • I still had a passion for code.
  • I actually had the drive to learn coding languages.

Career Interests

My interests focus on the fields of bioinformatics, genetics, entemology, and how they overlap. I've had previous experience working in these areas in various jobs. I've worked as a volunteer sorting insect samples and processing bee data; I've worked on an environmental stewardship project mitigating invasive plants species; I've done statistical analysis of gene sequences during the time I was working on my Bioinformatics Certificate; and I've created a project analyzing the potential relationship between the number of apple trees and number of squirrels in a neighborhood.

My main focus is on the interdependencies of biostatistics and entomology. Unfortunately, this does not seem to be a very large field at the moment. My hope is to help inspire more people to study insects and insect conversation using the tools made available by bioinformatics. We've already seen great leaps in the field of genetics because of these tools; why not apply them to other areas of science?

That being said, I'm flexible with working on any project as long as it improves my coding skills in some way.

Using All The Tools

There are a lot of understandable concerns about AI and its growing influence on the job market. I share many of these concerns myself. I'm happy to say that throughout my experience, I've found AI-backed assistant coding programs to be some of the most reliable forms of AI. They may not reinvent the wheel, but they're very efficient at speeding up the creation and de-bugging process.

This website was created with a combination of manual coding and suggestions taken from the Claude AI. No other AI has been used - I have written these paragraphs myself. I place great value on learning and understanding the basics of coding, and though Claude has been a great help, I've found I still need to know the fundamentals to comprehend what changes have been made and why. It's like using a calculator: you could just start typing random numbers in, but understanding what each function of the calculator DOES will enable you to solve more complex equations.

The AI programs I've had the most experience with are Antigravity, Google AI Studio, Google Gemini, and Claude AI. I also have two Microsoft Azure certifications (Azure AI Engineer Associate and Azure Data Science Associate); I use many of Azure's tools to create and train machine learning models, plan and manage AI solutions, and implement said solutions. This includes extensive work with running these apps on Google Cloud Run, creating Dockers, and performing jobs and services.
