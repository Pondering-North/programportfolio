Who I Am

Programming was not my first field of choice. Most of my school and college career was centered around the Arts and Sciences; I graduated college with a degree in evolutionary biology and ecology, not computer programming. But after a few attempted jobs, I found that I wasn't as interested in field and lab work as I thought I was; it was time to focus on an area in STEM that I felt better utilized my skills.

In August of 2025, I was employed as a data analyst and AI engineer. Though I was initally unsure about my ability to successfully understand programming, my experiences proved the opposite - I had both the passion and the drive to learn not just how to use programming languages, but to learn how to create apps that can help with everyday tasks and make life easier.

Career Interests

My interests focus on the fields of bioinformatics, genetics, entemology, and how they interact with each other. I'm mainly researching the interdependencies of biostatistics and entomology; the goal is to use the tools implemented in bioinformatics to enhance the study of insect conservation and genetics.

Using All The Tools

There are a lot of understandable concerns about AI and its growing influence on the job market. I share many of these concerns myself, but throughout my experience I've found AI-backed assistant coding programs to be some of the most reliable forms of AI. They're good tools to use when one needs to create an app quickly, or when one needs help debugging said app with less struggle.

This website was created with a combination of manual coding and suggestions taken from the Claude AI. No other AI has been used.

The AI programs I've had the most experience with are Antigravity, Google AI Studio, Google Gemini, and Claude AI. I also have two Microsoft Azure certifications (Azure AI Engineer Associate and Azure Data Science Associate); I use many of Azure's tools to create and train machine learning models, plan and manage AI solutions, and implement said solutions. This includes extensive work with running these apps on Google Cloud Run, creating Dockers, and performing jobs and services.
